The most significant advantage of the Internet is to break the geographical and time constraints. The idea of ‚Äã‚Äãonline language learning is the right combination of this advantage with foreign language learning. First of all, for those language learners with limited time (for example, professionals), a large number of language learning apps allow them to use the loose time for language learning. Most importantly, many online learning sites try to add a variety of activities to the classroom arrangement. These activities mobilise a variety of sensory channels such as sight and hearing, activate the whole brain. This can make the user learn a foreign language more efficient.

After all, online English learning relies on the network so there will be many temptations in the process of using the Internet. Especially for those younger learners, the purpose of education is not clear, curiosity is powerful, it is easy to neglect to learn because of other fun things. At the same time, for procedural online learning sites, curriculum design and traditional education are similar. Therefore, it is not possible to adjust the curriculum on time according to the learner's own study level, which is too rigid and stable and lacks autonomy. And the online courses offered by foreign teachers have exposed many problems in the process of teaching. First of all, the level and ability of teachers are mixed, which makes it difficult for students to choose. Second, many teachers are trying to attract students. The pursuit of novel and fun teaching effects, the introduction of a large number of stories or simple activities, but neglected the teaching of crucial knowledge points. Users can be attracted to interesting course content, and the learning process is spent happily, but there are very few knowledge points that can be truly understood.

Emotion Design

When using the product, the user has the opportunity to experience a variety of emotions. For example, they can get inspiration from the aesthetic look of a mobile phone, feel happy with new toys, be content with the high-quality sound of a music player, or be satisfied with the sound of an alarm clock. Different triggering conditions evoke these emotions, and they also differ in their behaviour and attitudes. Besides, positive feelings relieve stress and reduce the harmful effects of negative emotions. Thus, while we are considering how to combine physical props with emotion design, we can deliberately design products to elicit different and predefined positive emotions. In this way, the user is encouraged to support the use of products. For example, products that evoke happy emotions can stimulate new interactions, and products that generate interest can stimulate user-focused and exploratory interactions, and products that evoke user satisfaction can encourage peace and reflection.

Studies have shown that how learners attribute their performance influences not only their self-efficacy, motivation, and goal attainment but also their emotions. The motivation to insist on learning the language is the biggest problem faced by those who try to learn a language themselves. When they lose patience or learn motivation is weakened, they are more likely to have negative emotions that are tired of learning.
Besides, as Nakamura and Sachiko said that the Enjoyment was an emotion the most frequently mentioned by both groups while other emotions, such as joy, happiness, and disappointment, were expressed only by the beginner learners. As the current research shows, providing a good learning experience and making students feel happy and enjoyable is just as important as equipment. They use new L2 knowledge or exam strategies to increase motivation and improve attitudes, and in turn, assist them in further developing the second language.
