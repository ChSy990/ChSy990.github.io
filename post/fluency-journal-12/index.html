The Physical Block Discussion

The tags in the block can be divided into low frequency and high frequency using two different RF types. Our team needs to decide which way to use and how to install it into the block. Among them, low frequency (LF) tags have longer wavelengths and can penetrate thin metal materials more effectively. However, low-frequency RFID systems have a limited reading range of only a few centimetres or a few inches. Some common examples are access control systems and animal tags. High-frequency (HF) tags are suitable for reconnaissance of metal objects, and can also solve goods and medium water content. Typically, high-frequency RFID systems have a read range of a few inches, but can also have a maximum read range of approximately three feet (1 meter). For example, high-frequency RFID applications can track library books.

How to Choose a suitable RFID frequency for the game application

At the same time, it is similar to the radio, it must be adjusted to a specific frequency to hear different channels, and the RFID tag and reader should be tuned to the same frequency to communicate. There are three general types of RFID frequencies: low frequency (LF), 125 – 134 kHz; high frequency (HF), 13.56 MHz; ultra-high frequency (UHF), 433 and 860-960 MHz. We need to consider the scope of installation of concrete interaction equipment. Moreover, device size, if using low-frequency wavelengths, it is more difficult to use in Edge. This situation is very prone to problems that users cannot use blocks or texts that are unrecognizable, and it is easy to reduce the user's experience.

We have not been aware of the implementation progress and feedback results of RFID functions until now, so we cannot choose materials and blocks according to the specific size of the micro-controller. At the same time, we cannot continue to design the layout in The Edge's booth, because we cannot judge the actual range and distance that RFID can radiate.
