Critique

On Tuesdays and Thursdays, I also listened to other team presentations, some of which left a deep impression on me. The Gambit team's project is to help people who are sedentary in the office environment to provide rest opportunities. This type of user group needs to place "AMICA" on the desk and use lights to remind them to take a break or perform activities when needed. This is the team that I have been paying attention to since the beginning because their concepts are very close to the content of my thesis project, and I hope to get inspiration from their designs.

They have encountered problems similar to me if they keep users attractive and motivate them to continue to use them. Because the existing large number of reminder applications have the same problem, people are reluctant to choose to turn off or ignore these external prompts after hearing the alarm or message. So this requires designers to design and transform new features based on the environment in which they are used. First, they designed the entire device as an item on the desk and attracted the attention of the user through some attractive exterior shapes. At the same time, they also use a different colour to indicate the different states of the user. This is a great idea, and I think I can consider using a gradient light to remind the user of the current state of the body.

This inspiration comes from Zuckerberg's design that I saw in the news yesterday. The introduction of this product is shown in the video below. He is the "sleep box" designed for his wife. He created a wooden box that would shine, which would help his wife sleep better at night.

His wife, Priscilla Chan, after giving birth to two children, was not practical at night, and always worried that she would oversleep and miss the time to take care of her children in the morning, so she often woke up in the middle of the night to confirm the time and continue to sleep. Zuckerberg said that when her mother was tough because we had children, Priscilla could not sleep well all night. So specially designed a "sleep box" for his wife without time to help his wife adjust to sleep. When Zuckerberg designed the "sleep box", he deliberately did not display the time on the box. He believes that seeing time often puts more pressure on people. If you wake up in the morning, look up at the sleep box, it shows that it is only 3 am, then you will subconsciously give yourself a psychological suggestion: OOOO~ I can only sleep for 3-4 hours, it is frustrating.

Moreover, this timeless reminder "sleep box" will send out a weak and glare-free light when the child wakes up from 6 am to 7 pm, reminding Priscilla that it is time to get up and take care of the child. However, the glimmer of the "sleep box" does not affect the Priscilla in his sleep. At other times, the "sleep box" will remain unlit.

  1. Let the user put the block on the blank space of the sentence, which can strengthen the connection between the user and the system;

  2. The way user can recognize they have put the wrong block. We do not think about it. The result will be shown when the user makes a word at the end, which means the user can only understand whether they make the wrong/right word during the process, and they even do not know which part they put the wrong block.

  3. The teacher assistant is a good idea and also recommend us to discuss with someone who has experience in this area (classroom technology).
